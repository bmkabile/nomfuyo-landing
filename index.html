Strategic Preparation: Why This Program Works

Good nutrition ahead of mating and the strategic use of vitamins, minerals, and targeted drenches makes ewes fitter for conception and successful lambing. Combining this with timely vaccines **and strategic parasite control** reduces abortion risk, improves lamb survival, and increases conception rates. This is the practical, step-by-step program you need for success.

The Nomfuyo Mating Season Protocol

Phase 1: Pre-Mating Conditioning (8–12 Weeks Before)

Product / Action Timing Purpose
Feeding/Lick: Molatek Lamb & Ewe Lick (or equivalent) Start 8 to 12 weeks before mating. Continue feeding every time sheep return from the veld. Builds condition, energy, vitamins, and trace elements needed for conception.
Mineral Blocks: Elite Block / Multi Block Place in the **kraal or where they sleep** so they can top up minerals overnight. Ensures continuous mineral and vitamin intake to support fertility.
Images: Molatek Lamb & Ewe Lick, Elite / energy block (sample)

Practical notes: Spread blocks across the kraal to prevent dominant animals from monopolizing them, and always provide fresh water.

Phase 3: Strategic Parasite Control & Post-Mating Support

Internal parasite burdens can severely impact fertility. This phase is critical, ensuring ewes start their gestation period with a clean slate. We use a two-step drenching approach to target different parasite life stages and reduce resistance risk, while using injectable vitamins to boost overall health.

**Drenching 1 (Oral):** Ovidose 3 or Ovidose 4 **After mating start.** **10 mL** **12 mL**

**Mineral Maintenance:** Complex Forte **For 6 days once every month** during the mating window. Per product label Per product label

Best Practice: Ongoing Parasite Management

While the mating program addresses critical parasite loads, year-round vigilance is essential. **Do not drench without a plan.** We recommend using simple visual checks like the **FAMACHA score** in between planned treatments to identify the few animals that need individual attention, thereby slowing down drench resistance on your farm.

Always work with your local co-op or veterinarian to choose drenches based on known local resistance patterns and the specific parasites (like fluke) present in your area.

Phase 4: Post-Mating & Pre-Lambing

  • **Feeding Timeline:** Continue feeding lick and blocks until **one month after mating**. Reduce intensive feeding after that, and then **restart feeding more intensively two months before lambing** to build body condition for better milk production and lamb survival.
  • **Pre-Lambing Final Boost (4 weeks before lambing):**

    Repeat the **Complex A&E** injection. Administer either **Doraject AD3E**, **Ivermax AD3E**, or a final **OviDose 4** drench (depending on your local parasite risk and veterinary advice).

Conclusion: Consistency is Key

Following this program consistently is the best way to ensure high fertility, high conception rates, and ultimately, healthy lambs. By managing nutrition, controlling disease with timely vaccines, and reducing the parasite load, you set your flock up for success every mating season.
